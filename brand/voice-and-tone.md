# Webflow Brand Voice & Tone

## The Archetype

Webflow is **the mentor everyone wishes they had**: knowledgeable, experienced, and warm. We guide without condescending. We have deep expertise and share it generously.

## Brand Pillars

1. **Crafted for creators** — We honor the people who build for the web
2. **Powered by code** — Real technology under the surface, not shortcuts
3. **Driven by impact** — Outcomes over features, results over specs

## Voice Attributes

### Confident, not arrogant
We know what our platform does well. We state it plainly without hedging — but we never punch down or dismiss alternatives.

- **Yes**: "Go from prompt to production"
- **No**: "The only platform you'll ever need"

### Empowering, not hand-holding
We treat our audience as capable professionals. We give them superpowers, not training wheels.

- **Yes**: "Enable more teams to create"
- **No**: "Even beginners can do it!"

### Clear, not dumbed down
We explain sophisticated concepts in accessible language. We respect the reader's intelligence while removing unnecessary complexity.

- **Yes**: "Websites are built with three programming languages: HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. The Webflow logo is inspired by all three."
- **No**: "Our logo is techy and cool"

### Warm, not casual
We're approachable and human, but we're not your buddy. Contractions are fine. Slang is not.

- **Yes**: "You're ready to make your first API call"
- **No**: "Let's goooo! Time to hack some APIs"

### Substance over sensation
We avoid hype. No "revolutionary," no "game-changing." We let specific outcomes speak.

- **Yes**: "Achieve a 332% ROI"
- **No**: "Revolutionary AI that changes everything"

## Tone Shifts by Context

Tone is voice adapted to the situation. The voice stays constant; the tone flexes.

| Context | Tone shift | Example |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| **Marketing homepage** | Aspirational, punchy | "Smarter sites start here" |
| **Enterprise** | Authoritative, outcome-driven | "Drive pipeline growth with guardrails" |
| **Developer docs** | Direct, collaborative | "Click the 'Try it' button to make your first API call" |
| **Blog / editorial** | Thoughtful, educational | "Motion design isn't just eye candy; it's UX infrastructure" |
| **Customer stories** | Celebratory, metric-led | "7x faster landing page publishing" |
| **AI features** | Measured, control-forward | "Your data, your control. Always." |
| **Error / empty states** | Helpful, unflappable | Guide next steps, never blame the user |

## Sentence Structure

- **Lead with outcomes**, not mechanics. What can the reader *accomplish*?
- **Short, declarative headlines.** Save detail for body copy.
- **Parallel structure for authority**: "Design and build. Publish and manage. Analyze and optimize."
- **Implicit second-person.** "You" is understood, not overused. CTAs position the reader as the active agent.
- Contractions are welcome. They make technical content breathe.

## What We Don't Do

- Superlatives without proof ("best," "greatest," "most powerful" without data)
- Buzzwords as substitutes for specifics ("synergy," "leverage," "utilize")
- Hedging or apology ("We think maybe..." / "Just a simple...")
- Talking down to any audience segment
- Positioning AI as a replacement for human creativity — it's an *accelerant*
- Clickbait headlines — clarity over curiosity gaps
