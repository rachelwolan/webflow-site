# Writing by Surface

How the Webflow voice adapts to different contexts.

---

## Marketing (Homepage, Landing Pages, Product Pages)

**Goal**: Inspire action. Show what's possible.

**Register**: Confident, aspirational, concise.

**Patterns**:
- Headlines are short and declarative — one idea per line
- Feature descriptions lead with the benefit, not the feature name
- CTAs are active and specific ("Get a product tour," "Start for free") — never vague ("Learn more")
- Avoid feature lists. Frame capabilities as outcomes the reader achieves
- Use implicit second-person: the reader is the subject, not Webflow

**Headline formula**: [Outcome] + [where/how]
- "Smarter sites start here"
- "Go from prompt to production"

**Body copy**: One supporting sentence that adds specificity, then a CTA.

---

## Enterprise

**Goal**: Build trust. Demonstrate scale and governance.

**Register**: Authoritative, outcome-driven, specific.

**Patterns**:
- Lead with quantified results ("332% ROI," "7x faster publishing")
- Name recognizable logos for social proof ("Trusted by teams at IDEO, Monday.com, BBDO")
- Balance empowerment with safety: "autonomous publishing with guardrails"
- Vocabulary shifts to: "enterprise-grade," "unified," "extensible," "governance"
- Speak to business impact, not technical capabilities — the buyer already understands web platforms

**Key difference from consumer**: Assumes sophistication. No onboarding tone.

## Developer Documentation

**Goal**: Get developers building successfully, fast.

**Register**: Direct, collaborative, warm-technical.

**Patterns**:
- Imperative voice for instructions: "Click the Try it button," "Make your first API call"
- Contractions everywhere — "you're," "doesn't," "won't"
- Address the developer as "you" — personal, not institutional
- Progressive scaffolding: immediate action first, concepts second, advanced pathways third
- Short sentences. Scannable structure. Respect the developer's time
- Personality is allowed: "Get familiar with" instead of "Familiarize yourself with"

**Avoid**: "Utilize," "leverage," "please note that," or any phrase that adds syllables without adding meaning.

## Blog / Editorial

**Goal**: Educate and inspire. Position Webflow as a thought leader.

**Register**: Thoughtful, forward-looking, specific.

**Patterns**:
- Headlines use benefit-driven or how/why frameworks
- Colons pair conceptual hooks with concrete outcomes: "From audit to impact: How AI-powered SEO is accelerating organic growth"
- Content balances theory with implementation — insights paired with case studies
- No clickbait. Clarity over curiosity gaps.
- Subheadings are specific, not generic ("Why motion design matters for conversion" not "Introduction")

## Customer Stories

**Goal**: Celebrate customer outcomes. Inspire similar buyers.

**Register**: Celebratory, metric-led, customer-centric.

**Patterns**:
- Open with the quantified result: "18% increase in engagement"
- Follow a problem-to-solution arc: pain point, then transformation
- Success belongs to the customer, not the tool. The customer is the hero.
- Empowerment language: "took back control," "move faster," "freedom to move"
- Avoid discussing technical architecture — focus on workflow and business outcomes

---

## Pricing

**Goal**: Guide self-selection. Reduce friction.

**Register**: Conversational, practical, no-pressure.

**Patterns**:
- Each tier opens with a use-case statement: "Perfect for landing pages," "Ideal for blogs"
- Contractions ("don't") keep the tone human
- Avoid hyperbole — stress practical functionality and scale
- CTAs progress naturally: "Start for free" at lower tiers, "Talk to us" at Enterprise
- "Popular" label uses social proof without being pushy

---

## AI Features

**Goal**: Show capability while preserving user agency.

**Register**: Measured, control-forward, practical.

**Patterns**:
- Position AI as an accelerant for human creativity, never a replacement
- Repeat control language: "your data," "your control," "always"
- Specific capability descriptions over buzzwords: "actionable suggestions" not "intelligent insights"
- Acknowledge limitations honestly ("vary by workspace, site plan")
- Beta labels are visible and honest — position features as evolving
- Governance-first when relevant: "guardrails," "responsible use," "trusted vendors"
